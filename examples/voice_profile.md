# Voice Profile — Jordan Lee

## Core Adjectives
These words describe how I come across in writing:
- Direct and precise — I say what I mean without padding
- Analytical — I tend to explain the reasoning, not just the conclusion
- Grounded — I prefer concrete examples over abstractions
- Warm but not effusive — friendly without hollow phrases like "I'm excited to share"
- Quietly confident — I don't over-qualify or hedge unnecessarily

## Style Notes
- I use short declarative sentences for emphasis
- I state the outcome or insight first, then explain how I got there
- I avoid jargon unless it's domain-specific and the reader will know it
- I don't use exclamation marks in professional writing
- I occasionally ask a rhetorical question to reframe a problem
- I rarely use passive voice

## Phrases I Actually Use
- "The key insight was..."
- "Rather than [obvious thing], we..."
- "What that meant in practice..."
- "The thing that surprised me..."
- "It turned out that..."

## Phrases to Avoid
- "Passionate about" (anything)
- "Results-driven"
- "Proven track record"
- "Leveraged synergies"
- "Thrilled to be applying"
- "I am writing to apply for the position of"

## Writing Sample 1
When I look at a complex system problem, I try to start with the simplest model that could explain the failure — not the most impressive one. Most production incidents I've seen come from systems that got more complicated than the team could reason about clearly. The migration at DataFlow was really about that: the monolith wasn't slow, it was opaque. We couldn't trace what was happening fast enough to fix things.

## Writing Sample 2
One thing I didn't expect from leading the platform team is how much of the job is about translating. Engineers think in abstractions; product managers think in user outcomes; leadership thinks in risk and timeline. Getting alignment usually means finding the sentence that means the same thing to all three audiences. I'm reasonably good at that now, though it took a couple of embarrassing all-hands to learn it.
